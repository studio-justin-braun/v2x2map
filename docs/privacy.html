Privacy Policy — V2X2MAP

Last updated: 2026-05-26

V2X2MAP ("the app") displays ITS-G5 / V2X radio messages received from an external ESP32-C5 hardware receiver. This policy explains what data the app uses and where it goes.

Short version

The app works fully on your device. It does not require an account, contains no advertising, and includes no analytics or third-party tracking. No personal data is sent to the developer.

Location data

With your permission, the app uses your device's GPS location to centre the map on your position and to calculate the distance to received V2X messages. Your location is processed on the device only and is not transmitted to the developer or any third party. It is not stored beyond the current session unless you explicitly enable the optional "GPS track" or recording features, which save data to your device's app storage.

Optional MQTT forwarding

MQTT forwarding is disabled by default. If you enable it, the app publishes the V2X messages it receives over the air, together with a device identifier derived from the connected receiver's hardware (MAC) address, to the MQTT broker you configure. The default broker is operated by the OpenTrafficMap project (cits1.opentrafficmap.org); you may change it to your own broker or leave it disabled. The V2X messages are broadcast traffic data from other stations, not your personal information. The connection uses TLS.

Hardware access (USB / Bluetooth)

The app communicates with the ESP32-C5 receiver over USB-OTG or Bluetooth LE solely to read the captured radio frames and to flash firmware during setup. No data from these interfaces is sent off the device except through the optional MQTT feature described above.

Data stored on your device

Recordings you create (PCAP captures, CSV range/session logs, GPS tracks) are stored in the app's private storage on your device. You control them and can delete them at any time. They are never uploaded automatically.

Permissions

  • Location — map centring and distance calculation (on-device).
  • USB — communicate with the ESP32-C5 receiver.
  • Bluetooth — optional wireless connection to the receiver.
  • Notifications / Foreground service — keep receiving while the app runs in the background.
  • Internet — load map tiles and, if enabled, MQTT forwarding.

Children

The app is not directed at children and collects no personal data.
